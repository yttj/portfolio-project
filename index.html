Born in Hong Kong in 1960, artist Johnson Tsang employs realist sculptural techniques accompanied by his incredible surrealist imagination.

Tsang Yinquan is known for his kinetic porcelain and stainless steel works, from splashing sculptures to his weird faces twisted to express extreme expressions. Tsang was informed by his dreams and was particularly interested in depicting babies, which is a way to explore the perspective of the inner child. Although his work involves topics such as innocence and subconsciousness, it is also rooted in gloomy reality. Mr. Zeng, a former police officer, said that the darkness and violent crimes he encountered at that time left an indelible mark on his creative vision.
